Reflections of a Veteran Dual-Enrollment (DE) Teacher

February 4, 2024 | Jeffrey Elkner

Slides styled by Blu Medina

What is DE?

  • Dual enrollment is also concurrent enrollment
  • Students concurrently earn both high school and college credit

DE vs. AP

  • AP students earn college credit by exam for qualifying exam scores
  • AP courses are taught by high school teachers, not college instructors
  • The AP curriculum is prescribed by the College Board
  • DE students are in college classes
  • DE classes are taught by qualified college instructors
  • DE students earn college credit directly from the course, not from a single exam

Challenges of Being a DE Teacher

  • You serve not one bureaucratic overlord, but two! (and they often don't communicate well with each other)
  • The above fact can potentially lead to a lot of extra workload.
  • It also leads to a lot of extra credentialling requirements

Rewards of Being a DE Teacher

Personally, the joy I get from being a DE teacher is the main thing keeping me at retirement age from wanting to retire. I think that joy stems mostly from the increased academic freedom and creativity allowed me as a college instructor. I love planning curriculum and adapting it in real time to the needs of my students. As a DE teacher, I feel I can do that.

In speaking with SLA teachers during my visit here, I realized what I get out of being a DE teacher is similar to what the teachers here get from being in the SLA learning environment - the opportunity to both collaborate with the communitiy on shared goals and express their humanity through their classroom activities.
